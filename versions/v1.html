France

République Française

Architecture

From Gothic cathedrals to Haussmann's Paris boulevards. The Eiffel Tower, Notre-Dame, and Versailles define centuries of ambition in stone and iron.

Cuisine

The birthplace of haute cuisine. Croissants, coq au vin, crème brûlée — food elevated to art form, protected by tradition and terroir.

Italy

Repubblica Italiana

Architecture

Roman ruins, Renaissance domes, Venetian palazzos. The Colosseum, Florence's Duomo, and Palladio's villas shaped Western architecture forever.

Cuisine

Regional perfection: Neapolitan pizza, Bolognese ragù, Sicilian arancini. Italian cooking celebrates simplicity and the quality of each ingredient.

Spain

Reino de España

Architecture

Moorish palaces, Gaudí's organic fantasies, stark white Andalusian villages. The Alhambra and Sagrada Família represent two poles of Spanish imagination.

Cuisine

Tapas culture, paella, jamón ibérico. Spanish dining is social — small plates shared late into the evening, with wine and conversation.

Germany

Bundesrepublik Deutschland

Architecture

Romanesque churches, Baroque palaces, Bauhaus modernism. From Neuschwanstein's fairy-tale towers to Berlin's raw, rebuilt cityscape.

Cuisine

Hearty and regional: Bavarian pretzels, Rhineland sauerbraten, Black Forest cake. Beer culture is an institution, with over 1,500 breweries.

United Kingdom

The United Kingdom

Architecture

Tudor half-timber, Georgian symmetry, Victorian grandeur, and modern icons like the Gherkin. London's skyline is a timeline of empire.

Cuisine

Fish and chips, Sunday roast, afternoon tea. A cuisine of comfort, tradition, and an increasingly vibrant multicultural food scene.

Greece

Ελληνική Δημοκρατία

Architecture

White-washed Cycladic houses, ancient temples with Doric columns, Byzantine churches. The Parthenon remains the ultimate symbol of Western civilization.

Cuisine

Mediterranean simplicity: olive oil, feta, grilled fish, moussaka. Greek food is sun-soaked, shared generously, and deeply tied to the land and sea.
